3D Plot

# Persp
x <- seq(-10, 10, length= 30)
y <- x
f <- function(x,y) { r <- sqrt(x^2+y^2); 10 * sin(r)/r }
z <- outer(x, y, f)
z[is.na(z)] <- 1
# 0.5 to include z axis label
par(mar=c(0, 0.5, 0, 0), lwd=0.5)
persp(x, y, z, theta = 30, phi = 30, 
      expand = 0.5)
par(mar=c(5.1, 4.1, 4.1, 2.1), lwd=1)

Scatterplot

plot(life_expectancy, wellbeing, type='n', main="Life Expectancy vs Wellbeing")
points(life_expectancy, wellbeing, col="red", pch=16)
axis(1)
axis(2)
box()

In this scatterplot we see that life expectancy and wellbeing are related. Here we cannot imply causality but they definately seem to be corellated

Histogram of GDP per capita

hist(gdp_per_capita, main="GDP per capita", col="lightblue", border="darkblue")

The histograpm of the GDP is skewed with most countries falling in the lower GDP range of the dataset

Box plot of carbon footprint

boxplot(carbon_footprint, main="Carbon Footprint", col="lightgreen")

The carbon footprint of countries is represented as a boxplot where the box represents the middel 50% of the countries, the line inside the boxplot is the median. The whiskers outside show the variation in the data and finally we have the outliers as points.

Pie chart for Continents

pie(table(data$Continent), main="Continents Pie Chart", col=rainbow(length(unique(data$Continent))))
